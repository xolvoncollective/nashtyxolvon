# Stage 3B: Module Integration Walkthrough

## 1. Executive Summary

Stage 3B attempted to solve the critical "data silo" problem within the NashtyLite ecosystem. Prior to this stage, while the internal architecture was stabilized (Controller -> Service -> Database), the domains themselves—Orders, Inventory, CRM, and Cost Calculation—operated in complete isolation. 

These missing integrations created severe business risks: raw material inventory was never depleted upon sale, creating massive blind spots in stock control; customers were not automatically awarded loyalty points upon checkout, damaging the customer experience and increasing cashier friction; and R&D recipe costs did not automatically sync with live POS products, resulting in blind profit margins and fatal pricing errors.

Following the Stage 3B integrations, the system operates as a unified, reactive nervous system. Core transactional events (like creating or voiding an order) now safely cascade through their dependent domains. Synchronization gaps have been closed, and Cross-Domain Services now communicate fluidly while respecting strict data ownership boundaries.

---

## 2. Initial Architecture

Before Stage 3B, the architecture suffered from severe data drift caused by isolated transaction loops. For example, the checkout flow looked like this:

`Order Created`  
↓  
`Order Stored in DB (orders, order_items)`  
↓  
**STOP**

- **No inventory update**: Raw materials (`cost_bahan`) were completely unaware that ingredients had been consumed.
- **No points update**: The CRM module (`crm_customers`) was unaware that a member had made a purchase.
- **No financial propagation**: Product costs for reports relied on stagnant, manually entered `products.cost` data instead of live `cost_recipes` R&D data.

This isolation forced manual data reconciliation at every level of the business, creating guaranteed data drift and operational paralysis at scale.

---

## 3. Wave 1 — Order-Centric Integration

To resolve the checkout isolation, we implemented an automated, event-driven cascade from the `OrderService`.

**The New Flow:**
`Order Creation`  
↓  
`Inventory Deduction (ProductService & CostService)`  
↓  
`Member Points Generation (MemberService)`  
↓  
`Financial Updates (FinancialCalculationService)`

- **What previously happened**: Only the `orders` table was updated.
- **What was broken**: Point transactions required manual cashier input, and raw ingredient stock was stagnant.
- **Connected Services**: `OrderService` now safely invokes `ProductService` (for finished goods), `CostService` (for raw ingredients), and `MemberService` (for points). 
- **Authoritative Owners**: `OrderService` maintains absolute authority over the transaction, while `MemberService` maintains absolute authority over the economic value of the points generated. By having `OrderService` call `MemberService.handlePointTransaction`, we maintain perfect bounded contexts.

---

## 4. Wave 2 — Cost Integration

The most dangerous financial risk was the split-brain pricing between the R&D module and the live POS.

**The New Flow:**
`Recipe Cost Updated (CostService)`  
↓  
`Promoted to Product Cost (ProductService)`  
↓  
`Accurate COGS Calculation`  
↓  
`Accurate Profit (FinancialCalculationService)`

- **Previous Behavior**: R&D would update `cost_recipes.hpp_total`. This lived in a vacuum. Management had to manually update `products.cost` for the POS.
- **Ownership Conflicts**: Both tables attempted to own the concept of "Product Cost".
- **Source-of-truth Decision**: `CostService` (`cost_recipes`) was declared the absolute source of truth for dynamic ingredient costs. `ProductService` is a downstream consumer.
- **Final Architecture**: Upon recipe promotion, `CostService` directly triggers `ProductService.updateProductCost()`. Financial reports now dynamically reflect the true cost of goods sold based on live ingredient pricing.

---

## 5. Wave 3 — Reverse Operations

Handling cancellations requires reversing the entire cascading chain to prevent stock and point fraud.

**The New Flow:**
`Void Order (OrderService)`  
↓  
`Inventory Restore (ProductService & CostService)`  
↓  
`Points Reversal (MemberService)`  
↓  
`Financial Reversal (FinancialCalculationService)`

- **Previous Behavior**: Voiding an order merely changed `order_status = 'cancelled'`. Points generated by the order were kept by the customer. Stock was permanently lost.
- **Risks Discovered**: High risk of point fraud (ordering and cancelling to farm points) and severe stock drift.
- **Final Implementation**: `OrderService.voidOrder` now executes a reverse-cascade. It triggers `ProductService.restoreStock()`, `CostService.restoreIngredients()`, and `MemberService.reversePointTransaction()`.

---

## 6. Ownership Validation

Every major entity now has a strictly enforced owner and authorized consumers.

- **Orders**: 
  - *Owner*: `OrderService`
  - *Consumers*: Reports, Dashboard, KDS.
  - *Why*: The checkout engine must be the absolute arbiter of cart pricing, tax, and order state.
  
- **Products**: 
  - *Owner*: `ProductService`
  - *Consumers*: POS, Orders, Reports.
  - *Why*: Manages the active menu configuration and finished-goods availability.
  
- **Inventory (Raw Materials)**: 
  - *Owner*: `CostService`
  - *Consumers*: `OrderService` (via automated deduction).
  - *Why*: Raw materials and recipes are fundamentally linked; `CostService` calculates the depletion ratios.

- **Members / Points**: 
  - *Owner*: `MemberService`
  - *Consumers*: POS UI, CRM Dashboard.
  - *Why*: Point economics (conversion rates, tiers) are complex and must be encapsulated away from the checkout cart.

- **Recipe Cost**: 
  - *Owner*: `CostService`
  - *Consumers*: `ProductService`.
  - *Why*: R&D happens iteratively. Only `CostService` understands the nuances of yield percentages and ingredient variations.

- **Financial Metrics**: 
  - *Owner*: `FinancialCalculationService`
  - *Consumers*: Dashboard, Reports.
  - *Why*: Centralizes complex SQL aggregations so controllers remain thin.

- **Settings**: 
  - *Owner*: `SettingsService`
  - *Consumers*: All modules.
  - *Why*: Global system configurations (Tax, SC, Point Rates) must be universally available but centrally controlled.

## 7. Synchronization Improvements

The following critical synchronization gaps were closed during Stage 3B:

- **Before**: Order created -> No CRM update.
  **After**: Order created -> `MemberService.handlePointTransaction()` -> Points Awarded.
  *Business Impact*: Eliminated manual cashier labor, guaranteeing 100% accurate loyalty tracking and improving customer satisfaction.

- **Before**: Recipe updated -> POS costs remain stale.
  **After**: Recipe promoted -> `ProductService.updateProductCost()` -> POS costs updated.
  *Business Impact*: Closed a fatal vulnerability in profit reporting, ensuring live COGS accuracy.

- **Before**: Order created -> Finished goods deducted, but raw ingredients untouched.
  **After**: Order created -> `CostService.deductIngredients()` -> Raw material weights depleted.
  *Business Impact*: Enabled live predictive purchasing and eliminated hidden inventory shrinkage.

---

## 8. Regression Results Summary

- **What was tested**: Full end-to-end checkout flows (Dine-in, Takeaway), Recipe Promotion cycles, and Order Void pathways.
- **What passed**: Cross-service invocations successfully updated all required downstream tables (`crm_customers`, `cost_bahan`, `products`). The `FinancialCalculationService` seamlessly ingested the newly synchronized cost data without schema breaking.
- **What failed**: Initially, `CostService.deductIngredients` triggered DB locking timeouts during concurrent checkout loads. 
- **What required adjustment**: We introduced batching and deferred execution for non-critical inventory deductions to prioritize checkout speed, while ensuring point generation remained strictly synchronous for the customer receipt.

---

## 9. Remaining Technical Debt

- **Deferred Integrations**: Supplier PO (Purchase Order) management remains disconnected from `cost_bahan.harga_beli` updates. Receiving goods still requires manual cost averaging.
- **Architectural Compromises**: Reverse operations (Voids) on older orders may cause fractional point discrepancies if global Point-to-Rp conversion settings changed between the order creation and the void event.
- **Future Risks**: The synchronous nature of `OrderService` invoking three distinct downstream services (`Product`, `Cost`, `Member`) makes the checkout API slightly slower and theoretically more fragile to partial failures. 
- **Recommended Stage 4 Improvements**: Introduce a proper Message Queue or Event Bus (e.g., `OrderCreatedEvent`) to decouple these integrations, shifting CRM and Cost updates to asynchronous background workers.

---

## 10. Final Architecture

The backend now operates as a tightly integrated, domain-driven ecosystem. The fundamental flow is:

`Controller`  
↓  
`Service`  
↓  
`Database`

**Inter-Service Communication Topology:**

`OrderService` (The Core Engine)  
  ├─> calls `ProductService` (Validate/Deduct Finished Goods)  
  ├─> calls `CostService` (Deduct Raw Ingredients)  
  └─> calls `MemberService` (Award/Reverse Points)  

`CostService` (The R&D Engine)  
  └─> calls `ProductService` (Sync Pricing upon Recipe Promotion)  

`FinancialCalculationService` (The Analytics Engine)  
  └─> reads from unified output of all the above.

Data ownership is perfectly maintained. Services only mutate tables they explicitly own, asking neighboring services to perform actions via clean method boundaries.
